About Ric

Ric’s first public performance as a singer was at age six at sleep away camp for which he received a prolonged standing ovation and a then-whopping $20 tip for his medley of Al Jolson tunes, his first co-musical hero, along with Ray Charles, The Shirelles, Fats Domino, Mose Allison and Dave Van Ronk. (Some years later I introduced him to bebop jazz; he would eventually run a jazz club and produce many of the greats in concert.) Due to inner shyness defeating outward bravado, Ric basically retired from singing until the age of seventeen at which point singing, playing, writing and performing again took center stage as his passion, which has continued unabated to the present day. As a musician, he has been acclaimed as a virtuoso on the recorders and wood flutes. Ric also plays piano, percussion, various woodwinds and other instruments, a few not so well. (Let’s be honest, he was a god-awful violinist until giving it up, finally taking pity on his listeners) As a semi-adult, Ric performed worldwide (Europe, the Middle East, Asia, the Caribbean and North America) before crowds as small as three and as large as 10,000. That one featured a howling dog accompanying his flute solo, the main (ok, only) reason he was included on the bill. He was invited by the Cuban government to perform his original compositions all over the island of Cuba, which he spent one month doing- -singing and playing with many of Cuba’s finest musicians. At this point in his career as a performer, while Ric still plays the 88s and winds, he prefers to mostly sing for his supper. Although a folk/rock/blueser at heart, Ric has become more of a world and jazz vocalist over the last several years. He has had eleven plays produced for which he musical directed, wrote the music and sometimes the lyrics. These included collaborations with Sam Shepard, Howard Koch (co-screenwriter of Casablanca), Shakespeare and Chekov (the latter two without their knowledge or approval). Ric has had three albums released on Imagine Records. The first, (vinyl), KISSING BOOTH, received the highest rating and acclaim in a review in the New York Daily News; the second, (CD), MY MIND TOOK OFF WITHOUT ME, dealt with the inner voices which inhabit Ric’s psyche, and the last (DVD), ALL OF ME (ALMOST) is a visual compilation culled from a number of Ric’s concerts. His song/video, Bless Their Souls: Vote Hope, garnered more than 122,000 views on Youtube and was used in the successful 2021 Senate runoff campaigns for Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock. They wrote: “Thanks for creating the soundtrack for our path to victory.”RIC
